Field notes

Four threads that
keep my brain on.

Some are technical, some are tactile, all of them get a little better the more time I spend with them. Here's the long version.

All interests

01 · Cosmos

Space & the long now.

Astronomy Spaceflight Astrophysics

I think space is the closest thing we have to a free perspective upgrade. A single photograph of Earth from low orbit is enough to recalibrate a bad week.

I follow space missions the way some people follow football — Artemis, the JWST archive, the slow drumbeat of private launches. The technical details are fascinating, but the part I love most is the long-term thinking required to even consider going.

02 · Tech

Gadgets & quiet engineering.

Hardware Home lab Daily drivers

I'm into gadgets the way someone might be into kitchen knives — fewer, better, and used a lot. A well-made keyboard, a router that doesn't crash, a NAS that just works for years. That kind of thing.

It overlaps neatly with my day job: most of the gear I get excited about is also something I can learn from professionally. Tinkering with a home lab is, conveniently, how a lot of cloud and security skills get built.

03 · Travel

Travel as a refresh button.

Slow travel City walks Food first

I don't travel to escape — I travel to interrupt. Different streets, different dinners, different commutes. A week somewhere new is usually enough to rearrange the way I think about a problem at home.

My favourite trips are the ones with no big itinerary: a base, a few recommended places, and a lot of walking. Coffee, markets, public transit, repeat.

04 · Kitchen

Cooking, slowly.

Brazilian classics Weeknight pasta Sunday projects

Cooking is my analogue counterweight to a screen-heavy job. Heat, water, salt, time — the same four ingredients that have run the human kitchen for millennia.

On weekdays I'm pragmatic: a sharp knife, one pan, dinner in twenty minutes. On weekends I get ambitious — slow braises, fresh pasta from scratch, the kind of stew that needs three hours and rewards patience.
